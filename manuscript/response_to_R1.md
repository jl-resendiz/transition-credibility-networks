# Response to Conditional Accept (R1)

Dear Editor,

Thank you for the conditional accept and for the care with which you and the panel have engaged with this paper. I have addressed all four items. Each change appears in the revised manuscript; the relevant locations are noted below.

## Item 1: Romano-Wolf across the full hypothesis family

The robustness section (Section 4.5) now reports the Romano-Wolf stepdown procedure applied to the three-hypothesis family at the primary window [-1, +3]. The fuel channel survives all corrections (Bonferroni p = 0.000, max-t p = 0.000, Romano-Wolf p = 0.000). Neither the geographic nor the regulatory channel survives any correction.

Two clarifications on the family definition. First, the "9 hypotheses" referenced in the initial submission was a vestige from an earlier pipeline version that contained a bug in the bootstrap t-statistic computation; the corrected pipeline tests three channels at the primary window. Second, the event window sensitivity results (Item 2 below) confirm that the fuel channel is significant at all four windows tested, so including secondary windows in the hypothesis family would penalise for redundant replication rather than for data snooping. The rationale for the three-hypothesis family is stated explicitly in the manuscript.

**Location**: Section 4.5, paragraph "Romano-Wolf multiple testing."

## Item 2: Event window sensitivity

A paragraph now reports the fuel coefficient at four windows: [-1, +1] (t = -7.01), [-1, +2] (t = -7.88), [-1, +3] (t = -7.86), and [0, +1] (t = -7.43). The geographic channel is insignificant at every window. The result does not depend on the choice of event horizon.

I acknowledge the discrepancy between the prior response and the submitted manuscript, and I appreciate the Editor's interpretation that this was a drafting error.

**Location**: Section 4.5, paragraph "Event window sensitivity."

## Item 3: ESG qualification in the introduction

The introduction now reads: "ESG environmental scores are the stronger predictor of transition returns under pooled inference. Under Fama-MacBeth inference, both ESG and fuel-mix similarity survive a joint test." This matches the qualification in Section 4.3 and honestly represents what the data show.

**Location**: Section 1, paragraph beginning "An additional finding emerges."

## Item 4: Outlier diagnostic

Cook's distance analysis is now reported. Of 55,580 observations, 1,688 (3.0 percent) exceed the 4/N threshold. Dropping these observations strengthens the fuel coefficient from -5.51 to -6.35, indicating that outliers attenuate rather than inflate the estimated effect. This resolves the concern raised by the discrepancy between regression and portfolio-sort inference in R0.

**Location**: Section 4.5, paragraph "Outlier diagnostics."

## Additional changes

No substantive changes were made beyond the four items above. Minor copyediting corrections (punctuation, cross-reference formatting) were applied during the revision pass but do not affect any result or claim.
